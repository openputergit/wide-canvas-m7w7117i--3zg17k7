The 8 Limbs of Yoga

Ancient wisdom for modern practice

The eight limbs of yoga, or Ashtanga, provide a comprehensive roadmap for the yogic lifestyle. These guidelines help practitioners progress toward the ultimate goal of self-realization and enlightenment.

1. Yama

Ethical guidelines for moral conduct, including non-violence, truthfulness, non-stealing, continence, and non-coveting.

2. Niyama

Personal observances including cleanliness, contentment, discipline, self-study, and surrender to a higher power.

3. Asana

Physical postures that develop discipline, concentration, and prepare the body for meditation.

4. Pranayama

Breathing exercises that control vital energy and influence mental and emotional states.

5. Pratyahara

Withdrawal of the senses from external objects to achieve inner focus and self-observation.

6. Dharana

Concentration practices that develop one-pointed attention and mental focus.

7. Dhyana

Meditation, the continuous flow of concentration leading to deeper awareness.

8. Samadhi

The state of enlightenment, where individual consciousness merges with universal consciousness.
